# 🛵 JIOBP: Lead-to-Delivery Funnel Analysis

> **A data-driven deep dive into how JIOBP Mobility Centres turn interested people into happy, paying customers.**

---

## 📖 The Story Behind the Data

[cite_start]JIOBP runs physical stores (Mobility Centres) where customers can explore and buy top electric 2-wheeler brands[cite: 7]. [cite_start]But running a store isn't just about showing off bikes—it is about efficiently managing the customer journey[cite: 8]. 

The business wanted to know exactly what happens to a customer after they walk in or show interest online. [cite_start]My mission was to analyze the entire sales operation from start to finish[cite: 5]. 

I took raw data from **1,000 potential buyers (leads)** and tracked every step they took:
1. Becoming a Lead
2. Making a Pre-Booking
3. Confirming the Booking
4. [cite_start]Getting the Final Delivery [cite: 14]

[cite_start]By building an interactive Excel dashboard, I turned confusing spreadsheets into a clear story that store managers can actually use to boost their sales[cite: 5].

---

## 🎯 What Did I Actually Find? (The Big Insights)

I dug into the data across 4 major store locations (Bangalore, Chennai, Delhi, and Mumbai). Here is what the numbers told me:

### 🏆 1. We Are Closing the Majority of Our Sales
* Out of the 1,000 people who showed interest, we successfully delivered vehicles to a huge chunk of them, resulting in a **73% conversion rate**. 
* This means the sales team is doing a great job at the top of the funnel!

### ⏳ 2. Where Are Customers Waiting the Longest?
* [cite_start]I measured the Turnaround Time (TAT) to see how fast we move people through the process[cite: 15]. 
* Overall, it takes an average of **4.5 days** to complete a delivery. 
* **The Bottleneck:** The longest wait time happens right at the end—between the final "Booking" and the actual "Delivery" (which takes around 4.46 days on average).

### 💸 3. Why Are People Walking Away?
* Unfortunately, we still lose some customers. When tracking the cancellation reasons, I found two massive roadblocks:
  * **Price:** 61 people canceled because the vehicle was too expensive.
  * **Financing:** 59 people canceled because they couldn't get a loan or financing approved. 

### 😠 4. Customer Happiness Needs Work
* [cite_start]I calculated the Net Promoter Score (NPS) to see if our buyers would recommend us to their friends[cite: 19]. 
* The score sits at a very low **-53.42**. 
* A staggering **67.76% of customers are "Detractors"** (unhappy), while only 14.27% are "Promoters" (happy).

## 🛠️ The Solution: An Interactive Dashboard

[cite_start]To make this data useful, I built an easy-to-read Excel dashboard tailored for store managers and company leadership[cite: 22, 29]. 

**Key Features of the Dashboard:**
* **Lead Quality Tracking:** Managers can instantly see the breakdown of Hot (ready to buy), Warm, and Cold leads. About 50% of our leads are "Warm", meaning they just need a little push!
* **Clickable Slicers:** Want to see what's happening just in the Delhi store? Or only look at "Hot" leads? The dashboard filters everything with one click.
* **Visual Trend Lines:** Simple charts that track cancellation reasons over time so managers can spot problems before they get worse.

---

## 💡 How JIOBP Can Fix These Problems (Recommendations)

[cite_start]Based on the data, here is exactly what the business should do next to improve conversions and reduce cancellations[cite: 35, 37, 40]:

1. **Fix the Financing Gap:** Since almost 60 people walked away due to loan issues, ElectricPe needs to partner with more flexible banks or offer in-house EMI schemes.
2. **Speed Up the Final Delivery:** The 4.4-day wait from booking to delivery is killing excitement. [cite_start]We need to look at our warehouse and supply chain to get these bikes assembled and handed over faster[cite: 38].
3. **Save the "Warm" Leads:** 50% of the funnel is sitting in the "Warm" stage. [cite_start]The sales team needs to set up automated service follow-ups (emails/calls) to push these specific people into the "Hot" category[cite: 39].
